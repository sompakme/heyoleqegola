Where is my sprinkler master valve?

Answer

It is an automated valve (usually an electric solenoid type valve) that is put at the point where the irrigation system links to the water supply and controls the flow of water through it. The master valve is linked into a unique “master valve circuit” on the irrigation controller, which controls the irrigation system. (This circuit is referred to as a “pump start circuit” in certain circles.)

In the same vein, customers often inquire as to where they may locate their sprinkler master valve.

Start with that sprinkler and work your way down the line until you discover the valve. When searching for hidden irrigation valves, the most cost-effective and time-efficient method is often to probe the soil with a thin rod, such as a long screwdriver. Often, you may make an educated guess as to where the irrigation valve is located and then locate a concealed valve box by probing the earth.

Similarly, how do you determine the location of your sprinkler lines?

As previously said, locate the sprinkler heads (the points at which the water sprays out), and if you mark them on a plan of your property, you will most likely see a pattern and be able to determine where the lines go. The lines between the valves should be in straight lines to avoid any kinks.

Is it necessary to use a pump start relay?

The use of this device is absolutely necessary if you are using a pump to provide water to your irrigation system. A minimum of 15 feet between the timer and the pump is required for pump start relays to be installed to avoid electrical interferences that could cause problems with the pump.

Early Life and Education

CeCe Winans was born on October 8, 1964, in Detroit, Michigan, as Priscilla Marie Winans. She is one of ten children from a renowned gospel music family, The Winans. She grew up in a religious household that valued music, and her siblings formed a successful singing group named ‘The Winans’ that she later joined. CeCe attended a school for performing arts in Detroit.

Career

CeCe Winans became a renowned gospel singer-songwriter and recording artist. She released several successful gospel albums such as “Alabaster Box,” “Throne Room,” “Purified,” and “Let Them Fall in Love,” among others. In addition, she has won 12 Grammy Awards, 23 Dove Awards, and numerous other industry awards. CeCe Winans is a household name in the gospel music industry, and her contributions to the industry are significant.

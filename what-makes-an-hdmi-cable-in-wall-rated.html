What is the purpose of wall-rated cable?

In-Wall: Usually refers to a cable that is “in-wall rated” and can be safely put inside a wall. CL2 and CL3 are typically found on in-wall rated cables such as HDMI and Audio Video cables. Higher-level designations, such as CM, CMR, and CMP, are also available.

What is the difference between HDMI versions cl2 and cl3?

While both CL2 and CL3 must pass the same tests in order to be certified, the maximum voltage handling is the main difference between the two cables. CL2 cable can handle up to 150 volts, while CL3 can handle up to 300 volts.

What is CMR rated cable?

CMR cable, often called “riser-rated cable,” is constructed to prevent fires from spreading floor to floor in vertical installations. It can be used when cables need to be run between floors through risers or vertical shafts. Even higher rated is CMP (plenum-rated cable) (plenum-rated cable).

What is the difference between CMR and CMP cable?

Difference between CMP, CMR, CM / CMG, CMX cables. CMP cables are plenum cables; these cables are used if networking must be done in air ducts. CMR cables are riser cables. These are used for regular networking from room to room, as long as the cable does not need to pass through an air duct.

Save My Skin stars net worth trails behind skin wizard Dr Pimple Popper

Dr. Emma Craythorne, renowned for her work in the world of dermatology and her appearances on reality TV shows like Bad Skin Clinic and Save My Skin, has been making waves in the medical field. While she shares her skin expertise freely with the world, her net worth and family life remain somewhat of a mystery. Let’s dive into the details ahead of the eagerly anticipated Save My Skin season 5.

Dr. Emma Craythorne’s Path to Fame:

Dr. Emma Craythorne, at 46 years old, has made significant strides in the medical field. Originally from Northern Ireland, she pursued dermatology at Kings College Hospital London and furthered her expertise with a Mohs and laser fellowship at St John’s Institute Of Dermatology at Guy’s and St Thomas’ Hospital. She now serves as a consultant at One Welbeck, London, and operates her practice at 152 Harley Street.

Beyond her medical career, Dr. Craythorne is a TV sensation, starring in Save My Skin in the US and Bad Skin Clinic in the UK. She’s not just a TV personality; she also holds esteemed positions as the president of the British Cosmetic Dermatology Group and treasurer of the St Johns Dermatological Society. In 2022, she expanded her horizons by launching her custom skincare line, Klira.

Net Worth Comparison:

Dr. Craythorne’s net worth is an impressive $800,000, according to Cinemaholic. While this is certainly substantial, it falls behind her TLC colleague, Dr. Pimple Popper, whose net worth stands at a whopping $7 million. Despite the difference in wealth, Dr. Craythorne continues to excel in her field and make a significant impact on the lives of her patients.

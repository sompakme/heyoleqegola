Kenneth Va’a Niumatalolo (born May 8, 1965) is a former American football player and coach who now works as a football consultant. He has been the head football coach at the United States Naval Academy since the 2007 season, and he has been in that role since that season. In college, Niumatalolo competed in football for the University of Hawai?i. While playing quarterback for the then-Rainbows, he guided them to their first-ever playoff bowl game in 1989. He is just the second person of Polynesian heritage to be chosen head coach of an NCAA Division I FBS college football school, and he is the first Samoan to hold the position at any level of collegiate athletics. On January 23, 2014, Niumatalolo was officially inducted into the Polynesian Football Hall of Fame.
